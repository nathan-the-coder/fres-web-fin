The water cycle, also known as the hydrological cycle, describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth. Water moves through evaporation from oceans and lakes, transpiration from plants, condensation forming clouds, precipitation as rain or snow, and runoff back to water bodies. The sun's energy drives evaporation, converting liquid water into water vapor. When air rises and cools, water vapor condenses into tiny droplets forming clouds. When droplets become heavy enough, they fall as precipitation. Approximately 505,000 cubic kilometers of water falls as precipitation each year, with 398,000 cubic kilometers falling over the oceans.
